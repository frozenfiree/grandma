# Grandma's Hive — Visual Direction System

> Purpose: define ONE coherent visual language, then express it per business unit.
> The goal is **visual coherence**, not asset removal. Assets are judged against
> this system; anything off-system gets re-made to fit, in priority order.
>
> **Origin note (grounding):** the site is built on the **Strange Pixels** Framer
> template (`strangepixels.co`) — confirmed by shared DNA ("Make love, not noise",
> case studies *Wing / Fantom / Swisher*). So the target language already lives in
> the site's bones; this doc makes it explicit and adapts it to GH's four units.

---

## PART 1 — The shared system (applies to everything)

Coherence comes from these being **constant** across all units; only the *subject*
of the motion/graphic changes per unit.

| Dimension | The system standard |
|---|---|
| **Color** | Base **near-white** `#FFFFFF / #F8F9FA`; ink **near-black** `#111`; one hero accent **lime `#dbff00`**. Full **black `#000` is a deliberate "moment"** (editorial break, coming-soon), never the default. Per-unit secondary hue allowed but used in <10% of the frame. |
| **Typography** | Lay Grotesk (or licensed substitute): oversized **display headlines (weight 900, tight negative letter-spacing)**; **UPPERCASE labels/eyebrows** in `(PARENTHESES)`; lowercase for the big wordmark ("grandma's hive"). Big scale jumps = editorial hierarchy. |
| **Whitespace** | Generous and structural. Graphics **float inside whitespace**, not full-bleed clutter. Large vertical rhythm; type breathes. Whitespace is the canvas. |
| **Contrast** | High. Near-black on white, punctuated by lime. Occasional full-dark section for rhythm. No muddy mid-greys behind text. |
| **Motion** | **Purposeful and slow** — editorial, not stock-frantic. Signatures: horizontal **marquees/tickers** (scrolling statements), **fade-up-on-scroll** reveals, **subtle hover-scale** on media. 8–15s seamless loops. |
| **Shapes / geometry** | Clean and geometric (not organic/blobby): **rounded-rectangle media cards** (~16–18px radius), grid & baseline lines, nodes/plexus for tech, columns for editorial. |
| **Imagery vs graphics** | **Graphics/abstract motion** for section identity & backgrounds; **photography reserved for real proof** (team, product screens, real studio). No generic stock photos as decoration. |
| **Overlay** | Consistent: media sits behind a **soft scrim / gradient** with a **clear text safe-zone**; optional faint **lime tint** for unity; rounded mask. Text never fights busy motion. |
| **Signatures** | Scrolling **marquee bands** ("SAY HI!", "Don't Follow Trends. Feed Them."), oversized lowercase wordmark, **lime footer block**, **staggered/asymmetric work grid**, `(PARENTHESES)` eyebrows. |

**Reference anchors (current site):** the white hero, the lime footer, the staggered Work cards, the rounded video boxes, the ticker on the Services page.
**Reference anchors (Strange Pixels, `strangepixels.co`):** portfolio-first layout, oversized editorial type, marquee statements, whitespace-led composition, "streetwear × software" confidence.

## PART 2 — Per business unit (variations on the system)

Each unit keeps **all of Part 1**; only its *theme, motifs, motion subject, and a
hint of secondary hue* change.

### ▸ Gaandiva OS — *CRM · workflows · data flow · intelligence · pipelines*
1. **Themes:** unified customer data, pipelines, dashboards, intelligence.
2. **Motion:** data points travelling along lines; scattered records *merging* into one dashboard grid; gentle node pulses.
3. **Shapes:** connected nodes / plexus, pipeline lines, dashboard grids.
4. **Type:** system; eyebrow `(GAANDIVA OS)`; headline states the outcome ("one source of truth").
5. **Whitespace:** airy; the data-motion lives in a contained card with white margins.
6. **Contrast:** white base; a **dark data-viz card** as accent moment; lime on active nodes.
7. **Imagery vs graphics:** graphics/data-viz first; **real product screenshots** where credible.
8. **Overlay:** soft scrim + lime node highlights; left text safe-zone.
9. **Color:** white/ink + lime; **cool blue** as restrained data hue.
10. **Refs:** current `service1` network/data loops are *on-system*; SP product case pages.

### ▸ GTM Publications — *editorial systems · publishing · distribution · audiences*
1. **Themes:** newsrooms, headlines/typesetting, signal distribution to verticals (MarTech/FinTech/HRTech/CyberTech/SalesTech).
2. **Motion:** **type setting itself in**, columns assembling, a **headline ticker** (leans into the site's marquee signature), signals fanning to audience nodes.
3. **Shapes:** editorial **column & baseline grids**, distribution arcs to vertical tags.
4. **Type:** the **hero of this unit** — mastheads, big headlines, editorial scale.
5. **Whitespace:** magazine-like, strong margins/gutters.
6. **Contrast:** high editorial black/white + lime; a "press/ink" dark moment is allowed.
7. **Imagery vs graphics:** **typographic graphics** over photos; real publication screens where available.
8. **Overlay:** minimal — let type breathe; faint scrim only.
9. **Color:** ink on white + lime; a tiny color tag per vertical.
10. **Refs:** the site's existing marquee/ticker system; SP editorial type treatment.

### ▸ Simplified Management — *operations · processes · orchestration · business systems*
1. **Themes:** interlocking modules, process flows, orchestration, routing.
2. **Motion:** modules **clicking into place**, tasks **routing through a flow**, calm orchestration timeline — smooth, mechanical-but-quiet.
3. **Shapes:** modular blocks, connectors, flow arrows, kanban/grid.
4. **Type:** system; eyebrow `(SIMPLIFIED MANAGEMENT)`.
5. **Whitespace:** structured, grid-aligned, lots of air between modules.
6. **Contrast:** white base, crisp grid lines, lime for the "active/working" state.
7. **Imagery vs graphics:** **diagrammatic graphics**; dashboard screenshots where real.
8. **Overlay:** clean scrim + faint grid; clear text zone.
9. **Color:** white/ink + lime; optional **calm teal/green** as the "systems" hue.
10. **Refs:** `signature-tech-loop` (systems/network) is the closest current fit; SP systemized layouts.

### ▸ Podcast Studio — *audio · recording · production · media workflows*
1. **Themes:** waveforms, levels, recording sessions, on-air/broadcast.
2. **Motion:** **waveform pulsing**, level meters moving, on-air signal — rhythmic but slow.
3. **Shapes:** waveforms, concentric broadcast rings, mixer/fader geometry.
4. **Type:** system; eyebrow `(PODCAST STUDIO)`.
5. **Whitespace:** airy with a single focal waveform.
6. **Contrast:** the **one unit where a darker "studio/booth" base is on-theme**, with lime level-meters.
7. **Imagery vs graphics:** **mix** — real **studio/mic photography is appropriate** here (literal production) + waveform graphics.
8. **Overlay:** scrim; waveform sits behind text.
9. **Color:** ink/white + lime "record/on-air" accent; a warm studio tone permitted.
10. **Refs:** current `podcast.mp4` + the "studio warming up" coming-soon page; SP media work.

> **The 10 service/capability pages** (UI/UX, WebGL, Cloud, Brand, etc.) inherit the
> shared system directly; their per-card graphic just illustrates that discipline
> (WebGL = 3D mesh, Cloud = infra nodes, UI/UX = wireframe grid…).

## PART 4 — Sequence (after you approve Part 1 & 2)
1. **Approve the system** (Parts 1–2) — this is the gate.
2. Then prioritize replacements **by impact** (already drafted in `ASSET-REPLACEMENT-SPEC.md` / `ASSET-AUDIT.md`): Services grid → Gaandiva/GTM/Simplified cards → Service 6/2/3/5 → Hero/Banner → refinements.
3. Each new asset is produced **to this system** (use the per-unit motifs + the generation prompt), then wired in 1 line each, rebuilt, and rendered for review.

> Nothing gets sourced/generated until Parts 1–2 are signed off. The system is the
> contract; assets are just instances of it.
